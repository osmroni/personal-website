How I Think About Product

I see product management as the craft of making good decisions under uncertainty. My work sits at the intersection of systems thinking, data, and human judgment — understanding complex problems deeply before committing to solutions.

I care about clarity: clear problem framing, clear ownership, and clear measures of success. In data-heavy and AI-adjacent systems, I’ve learned that the quality of decisions often depends less on the tools themselves and more on how well the system is designed to support the people using it.

  • Start with the problem, not the feature — resist premature solutions.
  • Design for scale, optimize for learning — especially early.
  • Make tradeoffs explicit — ambiguity is inevitable, confusion is optional.
  • Build for humans, not dashboards — metrics serve decisions, not the other way around.
