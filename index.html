How I Think About Product

I approach product management as a balance between systems thinking and human empathy. I focus on understanding problems deeply before jumping to solutions, grounding decisions in data while staying close to users.I believe strong products emerge from clarity: clear problem framing, clear ownership, and clear success metrics.

  • Start with the problem, not the feature — resist premature solutions.
  • Design for scale, optimize for learning — especially early.
  • Make tradeoffs explicit — ambiguity is inevitable, confusion is optional.
  • Build for humans, not dashboards — metrics serve decisions, not the other way around.

What I’m Learning & Exploring

  • Data modeling and pipelines as first-class product decisions, not just technical implementation details.
  • How product managers can collaborate effectively with research and engineering in ambiguous, high-stakes environments.

I treat learning as an ongoing practice — combining hands-on experience, structured study, and reflection.
